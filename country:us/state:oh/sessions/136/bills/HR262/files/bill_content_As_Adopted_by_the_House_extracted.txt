A R E S O L U T I O N
Honoring Tallmadge Church on its Bicentennial.
General Assembly of Ohio are pleased to extend special recognition to 2
Tallmadge Church on the memorable occasion of its Two Hundredth 3
Anniversary; and 4
WHEREAS, This is an important milestone, and celebration is 5
certainly in order. Built by Lemuel Porter to house the Congregational 6
Church of Tallmadge and dedicated on September 8, 1825, Tallmadge 7
Church is a Federal-style structure featuring a two-story portico with 8
four large columns and a one-hundred-foot-tall steeple with a copper 9
weathervane. The building was donated to the Ohio History Connection 10
in 1971, two years after its congregation moved to a new edifice, and 11
today, it is managed locally by the City of Tallmadge, which rents it 12
out for weddings and events; and 13
WHEREAS, Those who have nurtured and sustained Tallmadge Church 14
merit praise for their remarkable initiative, diligence, and resolve. 15
With a wealth of vision and vitality, they have shown how very much 16
can be accomplished by a group of conscientious, industrious people, 17
and they can be proud that their unflagging commitment to education 18
and history has made it possible for the structure to survive; and 19
WHEREAS, Tallmadge Church has enhanced the quality of life within 20
the surrounding area, and its contributions to the local cultural 21
heritage have earned it the gratitude and respect of the entire 22
community. We are certain that this noteworthy landmark will remain a 23
fine representation of the area’s past for years to come; therefore be 24
it 25
RESOLVED, That we, the members of the House of Representatives of 26
