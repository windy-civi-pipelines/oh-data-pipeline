136th General Assembly
Regular Session S. R. No. 22
2025-2026
Senator Schaffer
Smith, Wilson, Wilkin, Patton, Johnson, Craig, Antonio, Blackshear, Chavez,
Cutrona, Gavarone, Hicks-Hudson, Ingram, Landis, Lang, Liston, Manning,
Reineke, Reynolds, Roegner, Romanchuk, Timken
A R E S O L U T I O N
To urge the President of the United States, the United 1
States Secretary of Veterans Affairs, and the United 2
States Congress to take prompt action to reduce the 3
processing time for veterans' disability benefit 4
claims. 5
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF OHIO:
WHEREAS, The service members of the United States armed 6
forces have bravely and selflessly served our country; and 7
WHEREAS, The United States Department of Veterans Affairs 8
disability benefits program provides monetary support to 9
veterans whose military service has caused or aggravated a 10
disabling medical condition; and 11
WHEREAS, According to the National Center for Veterans 12
Analysis and Statistics, the total number of veterans with a 13
service-connected disability has increased every year since 1991 14
and was 4,944,275 in 2019; and 15
WHEREAS, According to the U.S. Department of Veterans 16
Affairs, the Department took an average of 139.3 days to 17

complete disability-related claims in November 2024; and 18
WHEREAS, The U.S. Government Accountability Office made 19
several recommendations directed toward improving the U.S. 20
Department of Veterans Affairs' capacity to manage workload 21
risks, monitor and assess performance, and plan for further 22
development of information technology; and 23
WHEREAS, The "Veterans Appeals Improvement and 24
Modernization Act of 2017," 38 U.S.C. 7113, made changes to 25
improve the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs' appeals process 26
and also required the U.S. Government Accountability Office to 27
assess the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs' original plan to 28
improve the veterans benefits claim appeals process; and 29
WHEREAS, The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs has failed 30
to comply with the Veterans Appeals Improvement and 31
Modernization Act of 2017; now therefore be it 32
RESOLVED, That we, the members of the 136th General 33
Assembly of the State of Ohio, urge the President of the United 34
States, the United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs, and the 35
Congress of the United States to take prompt action to reduce 36
the processing time for veterans' disability benefit claims; and 37
be it further 38
RESOLVED, That we, the members of the 136th General 39
Assembly of the State of Ohio, urge the U.S. Department of 40
Veterans Affairs to adopt policies to ensure that, for each 41
applicant, the disability benefits program appeals process 42
concludes not later than three months following the date on 43
which an applicant receives an initial determination letter from 44
the Department; and be it further 45
RESOLVED, That the Clerk of the Senate transmit duly 46
authenticated copies of this resolution to the President of the 47
United States, the United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 48
