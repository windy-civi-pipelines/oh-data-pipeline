Sec. 4933.51. As used in sections 4933.51 to 4933.59 of 8
the Revised Code: 9
(A) "Area of last resort" means an area within an electric 10
distribution utility's designated service territory that is 11
located in a county of this state with a population of not more 12
than fifty thousand, but excluding any areas of the county that 13
are within a ten-mile radius of another publicly available 14
electric vehicle charging station or any areas of the county 15
that are within one mile of an alternative fuel corridor 16
designated by the federal highway administration. 17
(B) "Direct current fast charging station" means an 18
electric vehicle charging system capable of distributing 19

electric vehicle's rechargeable battery at a voltage of two 21

(C) "Electric distribution utility" has the same meaning 23
as in section 4928.01 of the Revised Code. 24
(D) "Electric vehicle" means a vehicle that is powered 25
wholly by a system that can be recharged via an external source 26
of electricity, including a vehicle for public or private use 27
that is a passenger car, commercial car or truck, a vehicle used 28
for public transit, a vehicle used in a vehicle fleet, a vehicle 29
used in construction work, and a vehicle used in industrial or 30
warehouse work. 31
(E) "Electric vehicle charging provider" means the owner 32
or operator of an electric vehicle charging station. "Electric 33
vehicle charging provider" excludes either of the following that 34
owns or operates an electric vehicle charging station: 35
(1) An electric distribution utility; 36
(2) An affiliate or subsidiary of an electric distribution 37
utility. 38
(F) "Electric vehicle charging station" means any 39
nonresidential electric vehicle charging system that is both of 40
the following: 41
(1) Capable of distributing electricity from a source 42
outside an electric vehicle to the electric vehicle; 43
(2) A direct current fast charging station or level two 44
charging station. 45
(G) "Level two charging station" means any electric 46
vehicle charging system capable of distributing electricity at a 47

infrastructure required to accommodate the electric load of an 52
electric vehicle charging station. "Make-ready infrastructure" 53
excludes an electric vehicle charging station. 54

distribution utility also shall file a proposal with the 95

publicly available electric vehicle charging station in the area 98
of last resort, and a description of the publicly available 99
electric vehicle charging station it proposes to construct at 100
the location. 101
(2) An electric distribution utility, concurrently with 102
the filing made under this division, shall provide conspicuous 103

(b) The date by which a person may file a proposal to 112
provide a publicly available electric vehicle charging station 113
within a ten-mile radius of the proposed location as described 114
in section 4933.54 of the Revised Code. 115
(E) Prior to approving an electric distribution utility's 116
installation and ownership of an electric vehicle charging 117
station under division (C) of this section, the commission shall 118
conduct a right of first refusal process. The commission shall 119

utility proposes to locate such a charging station. 123
(F) If, within ninety days after notice is provided under 124
division (D) of this section, no electric vehicle charging 125

under division (D) of this section, an electric distribution 128
utility may submit to the commission a notice of intent to 129
proceed with installation of a publicly available electric 130
vehicle charging station. 131

Sec. 4933.57. Nothing in sections 4933.51 to 4933.59 of 166

recovering the costs of make-ready infrastructure through rates 168
or charges authorized under the electric distribution utility's 169

nondiscriminatory basis. 173
